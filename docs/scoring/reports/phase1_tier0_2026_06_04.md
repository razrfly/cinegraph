## Phase 1 — Tier-0 categorical features

**Lever:** add dense, leakage-safe categorical signal already in the DB but never reaching the model.

- **In scope (implemented):** language one-hot (`lang_en`…`lang_other`, 13), genre multi-hot
  (`genre_action`…, 19), `content_rating_age` ordinal (1). Emitted by `Cinegraph.Scoring.DerivedFeatures`
  (train/serve symmetric, leakage-tested), catalogued `is_available: false` until the keep-criterion
  admits them.
- **Deferred — `keyword_ids` (Phase 1b):** ~50k distinct keywords → needs feature-hashing or top-K
  selection. User-approved deferral; the catalog/emitter scaffolding here is the template.
- **Deferred — richer honesty metrics (parallel task):** stratified recall@K, lift@top-x%, AUL. The
  A/B gate uses **PR-AUC** (the smooth iteration metric) by design — a group that doesn't move PR-AUC
  won't move full-pool recall@K, so this is the cheap pre-filter before any matrix/holdout spend.

**Pre-registered keep-criterion:** keep a group iff Δ PR-AUC ≥ 0.005 on ≥ 3 lists, and circularity
is not increased.

### Noise band (cross-seed stability of Δ)

Estimated from 3 seeds (1337, 7, 42) of the full A/B; the band is the per-group cross-seed std of Δ.

**Empirical noise band ≈ ±0.001 typical, up to ±0.011 on the small lists (genre/all).** The pre-registered keep-threshold of **0.005 sits at or above the noise floor**, so it's defensible (and for the noisiest small-list cells, any sub-0.005 Δ is correctly untrusted).

**Decision robustness:** survivors = `[]` on **all three seeds** — the null result is not a seed artifact.

**The one real signal, reproduced:** language Δ on the two consensus lists where it helped is well above noise and stable across seeds —

|---|---|--:|--:|---|

It is real signal that simply does not generalize across ≥3 lists under a single global switch — the exact case **Lever A (per-list/per-archetype feature admission)** exists to capture.

## What we learned

1. **The "wire up unused categorical signal" bet is largely spent.** Added linearly to the global
   per-list models, language/genre/rating do **not** move objective predictability. This *confirms by
   measurement* #1068's thesis that the structural/fetchable surface is near-exhausted — it does not
   overturn it.
2. **One real, sub-threshold finding:** language one-hot helps the two already-strong **consensus**
   lists (afi +1.7pp, NFR +1.3pp PR-AUC) but hurts 1001 and is flat on weak lists → **archetype-
   specific** signal, below the global generalization bar. We did **not** lower the bar to admit it.
3. **Per-list modeling already ran.** Each list was fit separately (the result table is per-list);
   the limit isn't "one global model" — it's that **feature enablement (`is_available`) is global**.
   The honest realization of per-list adaptation is **per-list/per-archetype feature admission**
   (Lever A) so afi/NFR can use language while cult/1001 don't.

## Revised next-slice priority

1. **Lever A — per-archetype feature admission** (cheap): re-test language *within* the consensus
   archetype, where it already helped.
2. **Lever C — Positive-Unlabeled reframing** (higher effort, highest literature-backed ceiling).
3. **Lever E — text embeddings** (biggest build; the one genuinely new channel).
